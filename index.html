About Keisuke Masuo

Founder & CEO of FOMUS — a cultural IP company expanding from traditional Japanese craft into luxury products, architectural spaces, and IP & media development.


Reinterpreting the 1,300-year-old "Masu" as a modern luxury product, while applying its construction to architectural and spatial design. Through the fantasy IP "KUKU" and a professional production studio, Masuo expands Japanese cultural assets into global markets. Driven by a four-layer structure of PRODUCT × SPACE × IP × COMMUNITY, he is building a scalable cultural brand originating from Japan.

Group Structure

FOMUS Group Structure

FOMUS currently operates across four business portfolios.

01
Luxury Craft Division

Reinterpreting the traditional Japanese craft "Masu" for the modern luxury market. Driving high value-add, exclusivity, and brand asset formation.

Masu is a traditional Japanese wooden box originally used to measure rice, with over 1,300 years of history. FOMUS applies a proprietary coating technology that enables waterproof and heat-resistant functionality, transforming this cultural artifact into a modern luxury product.
